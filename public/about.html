About ChitoNet

An innovative IoT solution for early detection and prevention of eutrophication in aquatic ecosystems

Understanding Eutrophication

Eutrophication is a natural process that becomes problematic when accelerated by human activities. It occurs when water bodies receive excessive nutrients, particularly nitrogen and phosphorus compounds, leading to an overgrowth of algae and aquatic plants.

This nutrient overload causes rapid algal blooms that deplete oxygen levels, create "dead zones" where aquatic life cannot survive, and produce harmful toxins. The consequences include:

⚠

Ecosystem Collapse

Loss of biodiversity and aquatic life

💧

Water Quality

Unsafe water for consumption and recreation

💰

Economic Impact

Costly cleanup and lost fisheries revenue

🌍

Climate Effects

Increased greenhouse gas emissions

The ChitoNet Solution

Early Detection System

ChitoNet employs a network of intelligent buoys equipped with Arduino Uno R4 WiFi microcontrollers and three critical sensors: pH sensors, water temperature sensors (DS18B20), and turbidity sensors. These buoys are strategically deployed across large water areas, forming a comprehensive monitoring network that can detect early warning signs of eutrophication.

Real-Time Monitoring

Each buoy continuously measures and transmits data about:

  • • pH Levels: Detecting acidity/alkalinity changes that indicate chemical imbalances
  • • Water Temperature: Monitoring thermal variations that can accelerate nutrient absorption and algal growth
  • • Turbidity: Measuring suspended particles and water clarity to identify sediment, algae, or organic matter presence

Distributed Network Architecture

Unlike single-point monitoring systems, ChitoNet operates as a distributed network of buoys. These buoys are placed across large water bodies—like a monitoring net—allowing for spatial analysis of water quality. This network approach enables:

  • ✓ Identification of problem areas within larger water bodies
  • ✓ Comparison of water quality across different zones
  • ✓ Early detection of localized eutrophication before it spreads
  • ✓ Real-time data from multiple locations simultaneously

Chitosan-Based Prevention

When sensors detect parameters indicating potential eutrophication (such as rising pH, increasing turbidity, or temperature anomalies), ChitoNet enables proactive intervention using chitosan mixtures.

What is Chitosan?

Chitosan is a natural biopolymer derived from chitin, found in the shells of crustaceans. It's an eco-friendly, biodegradable material that has shown remarkable effectiveness in preventing eutrophication.

How It Works:

  • 1
    Nutrient Binding: Chitosan molecules bind with excess phosphorus and nitrogen compounds in the water, making them unavailable to algae.
  • 2
    Prevention: By removing the primary food source for algae, chitosan prevents algal blooms from forming in the first place.
  • 3
    Safety: Unlike chemical treatments, chitosan is biodegradable, non-toxic, and safe for aquatic ecosystems.

The system can automatically trigger chitosan mixture deployment in affected areas when threats are detected, providing immediate preventive action while continuing to monitor water quality post-treatment.
